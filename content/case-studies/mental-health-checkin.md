## Overview

A mobile-first daily wellness tool designed for college students, created in collaboration with UCSD Student Health Services.

## Problem

Existing mental health apps feel clinical and transactional. Students abandon them within weeks. The challenge was to design something students would actually return to.

## Research Methods

- Diary studies with 8 participants over 3 weeks
- Co-design workshops with student wellbeing advocates
- Competitive analysis of 10 existing apps

## Key Findings

Students wanted acknowledgement, not advice. The most-loved interactions were ones that simply said "we heard you" without immediately suggesting resources or solutions.

## Design Decisions

- **Micro check-ins**: 30-second daily prompts rather than long forms
- **No streaks**: Removed gamification that created guilt when broken
- **Warm language**: Tone-of-voice guidelines written with a counsellor

## Results

Pilot with 60 students over 6 weeks showed 73% retention at week 4, compared to 28% industry average for wellness apps.
