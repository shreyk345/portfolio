## Overview

A research-driven VR experience developed in collaboration with the UCSD School of Medicine, designed to cultivate empathy in pre-clinical students.

## Background

Studies show that empathy in medical providers directly correlates with patient outcomes. Yet empathy scores reliably drop during medical school.

## My Role

I led UX research, interaction design for the VR environment, and post-experience debrief interface design.

## Process

- **Discovery:** 8 interviews with chronic pain patients and 6 with medical educators
- **Concept testing:** Paper prototypes of the debrief UI with 10 students
- **Iteration:** 4 rounds of playtesting the VR scenario

## Key Design Decisions

The experience is first-person and narrated in the patient's own words. The debrief UI surfaces emotional moments from the session and prompts structured reflection.

## Results

Post-experience surveys showed a statistically significant increase in empathy scores. The project was presented at the 2024 UCSD Design Symposium.
