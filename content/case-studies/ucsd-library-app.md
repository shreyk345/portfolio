## Overview

This project was a semester-long UX research and design initiative to reimagine how students interact with the UCSD library system on mobile.

## The Problem

First-year students consistently reported feeling lost — both physically in the library stacks, and mentally when trying to navigate the existing app.

## Research

I conducted 12 user interviews, 3 contextual inquiry sessions, and a competitive analysis of 6 library apps at peer institutions.

**Key findings:**
- 78% of users abandoned the app within 30 seconds
- The search results gave no physical wayfinding cues
- Reservation flows required too many taps

## Solution

I designed an AR wayfinding overlay that guides users directly to their book's shelf location, and a simplified reservation flow reduced from 7 steps to 3.

## Outcome

Prototype testing showed a 64% improvement in task completion time and a measurable reduction in reported stress levels.
